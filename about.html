About SutraBooks

 Sūtra focuses on creative work originating in the Himalayas. We cover all forms, from the sūtra to everything else on the literary spectrum, and especially foster content inspired by Buddhism. We actively publish work that syncs with our vision.

 An annual award, judged by a group of notable writers, journalists, filmmakers and academics, is presented to distinguished writing published by Sūtra. Our annual writers’ festival seeks to support established and emerging talent in the region.

 We welcome writers of all stripes. Unsolicited submissions of fiction, nonfiction, and poetry, among others, are accepted. We ask one thing: that the work be inspired by or be of Himalayan and Buddhist provenance. Writers are requested to submit no more than one short story, one nonfiction piece, or up to three poems at a time to sutrabooks@gmail.com.

 Tsering Namgyal is the founder-editor of Sūtra. He is a journalist and the author of three books, Little Lhasa: Reflections on Exiled Tibet, His Holiness the 17th Karmapa Ogyen Trinley Dorjee: A biography and The Tibetan Suitcase: A Novel. He is based in Dehra Dun.

Abhyudai Dhawan is a contributing editor.
